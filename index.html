Liveability in Australia

As populations of metropolitan cities increase rapidly, living conditions within those cities are more important than ever. Liveability describes the frame conditions of a decent life for all citizens of the city. The Economic Intelligence Unit (EIU) creates a Global Liveability Index examines 140 cities worldwide and creates a ranking of their liveability, by the Liveability Index. Australian cities consistently rank as some of the most liveable cities. In particular Melbourne was awarded the world’s most liveable city for the seventh reoccurring year in 2017. What impacts this, and how is liveability in Australian capitals?

What positively impacts liveability?

The EIU considers many factors in their ranking. These factors are culture, education, health care, infrastructure, and stability. In a survey done by the City of Melbourne, it was found that citizens of the city greatly value health care and infrastructure, similar to the EIU.

What factors negatively impact liveability?

Many factors also decrease liveability. According to the same survey, residents of Melbourne expressed that factors that decrease the liveability is the high cost of living, lack of safety, pollution, and weather. Notably, cost of living and lack of safety are significantly more important than pollution and weather.

Effect of the pandemic on liveability

Obviously, the pandemic has created a huge shift in living standards and situations. There was a significant drop off in the Liveability Index of Australian capitals as a result, with larger cities being affected more.

Top suburbs in Melbourne

Even with the pandemic, Melbourne suburbs are still amongst some of the best places to live. PwC’s CityPulse ranks Melbourne suburbs by a City Pulse Score, from 0 to 10, measuring the living conditions. As seen, the eastern suburbs rank extremely high.
